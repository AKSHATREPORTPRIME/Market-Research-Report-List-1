<p><p>Semiconductor wafer electrostatic chucks (ESC) are used in semiconductor manufacturing processes to hold wafers in place. There are two main types of ESCs in the market: Coulomb Type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type. Coulomb Type ESCs use the principle of electrical charge attraction to hold the wafer, while JR Type ESCs utilize a combination of electrical charge attraction and Bernoulli's principle to achieve better wafer handling capabilities. Both types offer different advantages and are chosen based on specific requirements in semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>
