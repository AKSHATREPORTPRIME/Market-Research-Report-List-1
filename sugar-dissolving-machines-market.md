<p><p>The sugar dissolving machines market offers two main types of dissolution methods: batch dissolution and continuous dissolution. In batch dissolution, a fixed quantity of sugar is dissolved in a specific volume of liquid at a time. This method is commonly used in small-scale applications and allows for better control over the dissolution process. On the other hand, continuous dissolution involves a continuous flow of sugar and liquid, enabling large-scale production with an uninterrupted dissolving process. It is often employed in industrial settings and offers high efficiency and productivity.</p></p>
