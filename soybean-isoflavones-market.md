<p><p>Soybean isoflavones are bioactive compounds found in soybeans that have gained attention due to their potential health benefits. There are three main types of soybean isoflavones: genistein, daidzein, and glucitein. Genistein is the most abundant and extensively studied isoflavone, known for its antioxidant and estrogen-like properties. Daidzein also exhibits antioxidant and estrogenic activities, while glucitein is less common and has similar properties to the other two. These isoflavones have attracted interest in the market as dietary supplements for various health conditions, although their efficacy and safety are still being researched.</p></p>
