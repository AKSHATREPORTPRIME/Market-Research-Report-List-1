<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The commercial aircraft battery market consists of three main types: lithium-based battery, nickel-based battery, and lead acid battery. Lithium-based batteries are lightweight, have high energy density, and long operational life, making them the most widely used in modern aircraft. Nickel-based batteries have a good power-to-weight ratio but are less commonly used due to their lower energy density. Lead acid batteries are heavy and have lower energy density but are still used in smaller aircraft due to their affordability and reliability.</p></p>

<p><p>The commercial aircraft battery market encompasses various applications, including the main battery and APU (Auxiliary Power Unit) battery. The main battery serves as the primary power source for critical systems like starting the engines, emergency lighting, and backups during electrical failures. On the other hand, APU batteries are specifically designed to power the aircraft's APU, which provides electrical power when the engines are not running, including during ground operations. Both batteries are crucial components that ensure the reliable operation and safety of commercial aircraft.</p></p>
