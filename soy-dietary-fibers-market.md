<p><strong>The Soy Dietary Fibers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Soy Dietary Fibers market can be categorized into Type I and Type II. Type I refers to soy dietary fibers that are extracted from the defatted soybean flour. These fibers are predominantly composed of cellulose and hemicellulose. On the other hand, Type II soy dietary fibers are extracted from the whole soybean and are a mixture of soluble and insoluble fibers. Type II fibers contain more non-cellulosic polysaccharides, such as pectins and gums, offering additional health benefits beyond the cellulose and hemicellulose found in Type I fibers.</p></p>
