<p><p>The aviation carbon fiber market consists of two main types: continuous fiber and chopped fiber. Continuous fiber refers to long, unbroken strands of carbon fiber that are used to reinforce various aviation components such as wings, fuselages, and engine parts. This type of fiber offers high strength and stiffness, making it ideal for applications requiring optimum performance. On the other hand, chopped fiber refers to shorter lengths of carbon fiber that are mixed with other materials to form composite materials. Chopped fiber is commonly used in non-structural aviation parts such as interior panels and fairings.</p></p>
