<p><p>The vacuum forming plastic market can be divided into two types: machinable and not machinable. Machinable vacuum forming plastics are designed to be easily shaped, cut, and modified using standard tools and machinery. They offer the advantage of flexibility and versatility for customization. On the other hand, not machinable vacuum forming plastics are more rigid and cannot be easily modified using conventional methods. These plastics are typically used in applications where precision and uniformity are crucial, such as in automotive or aerospace industries.</p></p>
