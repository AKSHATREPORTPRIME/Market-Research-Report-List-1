<p><p>Metallized papers refer to specially treated papers coated with a thin layer of metal, enhancing their visual appearance, durability, and barrier properties. Two commonly used types of metallization in the market are lamination type and vacuum metallization type. Lamination type involves bonding a layer of metallic film onto the paper's surface using an adhesive, providing a shiny finish and moisture resistance. Vacuum metallization type, on the other hand, applies a metal coating on the paper through a vacuum process, resulting in a more reflective and higher barrier material suitable for various packaging and labeling purposes.</p></p>
