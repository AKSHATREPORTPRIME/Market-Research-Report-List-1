<p><p>Leadless cardiac pacemakers are small devices implanted without the need for leads or wires. They are designed to regulate the electrical impulses of the heart and maintain a normal heart rate. Traditional pacemakers consist of a generator and one or more leads that are threaded through the veins into the heart. In contrast, leadless pacemakers are directly implanted into the heart, delivering electrical signals to regulate the heart's rhythm.</p><p>The future outlook for the leadless cardiac pacemakers market is promising. The increasing prevalence of cardiac disorders, technological advancements, and the rising demand for minimally invasive surgeries are anticipated to drive market growth. Leadless pacemakers offer several advantages over traditional pacemakers, including reduced risk of infection, improved patient comfort, and simplified implantation procedures.</p><p>The growing elderly population, which is more susceptible to heart diseases, is also expected to contribute to market growth. Furthermore, the shift towards value-based healthcare and cost-effectiveness of leadless pacemakers compared to conventional devices will further propel market expansion.</p><p>However, challenges such as the high cost of leadless pacemakers, limited reimbursement policies, and the requirement of technically skilled professionals for implantation may hinder market growth to some extent.</p><p>Overall, the leadless cardiac pacemakers market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.4% during the forecast period.
