<p><p>Retardant solvent is categorized into three types: organohalogen compounds, organophosphate compounds, and others. Organohalogen compounds are solvents that contain halogen elements, such as chlorine or bromine, in their molecular structure. Organophosphate compounds, on the other hand, include solvents that contain organic compounds combined with phosphate. The "others" category refers to all other types of retardant solvents that do not fall into the previous two categories, which could include solvents with different chemical compositions or combinations. These different types of retardant solvents are used for various applications, depending on their specific properties and characteristics.</p></p>
