<p><p>Radiation-cured coatings are a type of coating that are cured or dried using high-energy radiation instead of traditional heat or solvents. These coatings are divided into two main types - Ultraviolet (UV) curing and Electron Beam (EB) curing. UV curing involves the use of UV light to initiate a chemical reaction that hardens the coating. On the other hand, EB curing utilizes high-energy electrons to cure the coating. Both methods offer benefits such as rapid curing, high productivity, and environmentally friendly characteristics, making them popular in various applications.</p></p>
