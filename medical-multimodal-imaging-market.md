<p><ul><li>PET-CT</li><li>SPECT-CT</li><li>PET-MR</li><li>OCT</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical multimodal imaging refers to the simultaneous use of multiple imaging techniques in order to obtain a comprehensive understanding of a patient's medical condition. The market for such imaging includes various types, such as PET-CT, SPECT-CT, PET-MR, OCT, and others. PET-CT combines positron emission tomography (PET) and computed tomography (CT) to provide both functional and anatomical information. SPECT-CT combines single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) and CT, offering detailed images of organs and tissues. PET-MR combines PET and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) for improved soft tissue contrast. OCT, or optical coherence tomography, uses light waves to capture high-resolution images of tissues, particularly in ophthalmology. Other multimodal imaging techniques include fusion imaging, elastography, and fluorescence imaging.</p></p>
