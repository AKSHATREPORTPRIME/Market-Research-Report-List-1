<p><p>The Kidney Cancer Molecular Diagnostics Market encompasses various types of tests used to diagnose and understand kidney cancer at a molecular level. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a technique that amplifies and detects cancer-specific genetic material. Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) involves labeling specific genes to identify genetic abnormalities. DNA sequencing is the process of determining the order of nucleotides in a DNA molecule, helping identify mutations. Gene chips are microarrays that analyze gene expression patterns. These diagnostic tools aid in early detection, treatment selection, and monitoring of kidney cancer.</p></p>
