<p><p>Cranial orthoses refer to devices, such as helmets, used to treat skull deformities in infants. Active helmets incorporate advanced mechanisms, often involving springs or hinges, that apply controlled pressure to reshape the skull. They offer adjustable and personalized treatment. Conversely, passive helmets are non-adjustable and exert uniform pressure on the skull to guide growth. Active helmets provide more flexibility for custom treatment, while passive helmets are simpler and offer more cost-effective options. Both types aim to correct skull deformities in infants, but differ in their approach and level of customization.</p></p>
