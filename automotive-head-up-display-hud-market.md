<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The Automotive Head-Up-Display (HUD) market comprises two main types: Windshield Projected HUD and Combiner Projected HUD. Windshield Projected HUD uses the windshield as a display surface, directly projecting information onto it, allowing drivers to see critical information while keeping their focus on the road. On the other hand, Combiner Projected HUD uses a small transparent glass panel, placed in front of the driver, to reflect a virtual image, displaying information such as speed, navigation, and warnings, without obstructing the driver's view.</p></p>
