<p><p>Glucan is a type of dietary fiber found in plants, particularly in grains like oats and barley. There are two main types of glucan in the market: soluble and insoluble. Soluble glucan dissolves in water to form a gel-like substance in the digestive tract, aiding in lowering cholesterol levels and regulating blood sugar. Insoluble glucan, on the other hand, does not dissolve and adds bulk to the stool, promoting regular bowel movements and preventing constipation. Both types have their own health benefits and are commonly used as food additives or dietary supplements.</p></p>
