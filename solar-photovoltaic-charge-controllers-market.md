<p><p>Solar photovoltaic charge controllers are devices used in solar power systems to regulate the charging and discharging of batteries. Two common types of charge controllers are PWM (Pulse-Width Modulation) and MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking). PWM controllers adjust the charging current by rapidly switching the solar panels on and off, while MPPT controllers use advanced algorithms to track the maximum power output of the solar panels and optimize the charging process accordingly. PWM controllers are more affordable but less efficient, while MPPT controllers are pricier but offer higher conversion efficiency and improved system performance.</p></p>
