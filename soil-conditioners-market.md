<p><p>Soil conditioners are products used to improve the quality and fertility of soil. There are two main types of soil conditioners: natural and synthetic. Natural soil conditioners are derived from organic sources such as compost, manure, and peat moss. They help retain moisture, enhance nutrient content, and promote healthy microbial activity in the soil. On the other hand, synthetic soil conditioners are chemically engineered products that mimic the properties of natural conditioners. They are designed to improve soil structure, increase water-holding capacity, and enhance nutrient availability for plants.</p></p>
