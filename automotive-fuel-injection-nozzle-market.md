<p><p>The automotive fuel injection nozzle market consists of two types: non-cooled type and cooled type. Non-cooled type nozzles are designed without any cooling mechanism, making them simpler and less expensive. They are commonly used in entry-level vehicles with lower fuel injection pressures. In contrast, cooled type nozzles feature a cooling system to handle higher fuel injection pressures. They are typically found in high-performance vehicles and offer better reliability and fuel atomization. The choice between these types depends on the vehicle's performance requirements and budget considerations.</p></p>
