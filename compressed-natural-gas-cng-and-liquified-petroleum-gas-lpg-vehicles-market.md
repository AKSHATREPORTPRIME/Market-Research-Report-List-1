<p><p>Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) vehicles are automobiles that use natural gas as a fuel source, which is stored under high pressure in a tank. These vehicles have combustion engines that are specifically designed to run on natural gas, thereby reducing emissions and providing a cleaner alternative to gasoline or diesel-powered vehicles.</p><p>Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) vehicles, on the other hand, use a mixture of propane and butane as a fuel source. LPG is stored under pressure in a tank and is used as an alternative fuel for combustion engines in vehicles. LPG vehicles produce lower emissions compared to their gasoline or diesel counterparts.</p><p>Both CNG and LPG vehicles are gaining popularity due to their environmental benefits and cost savings. CNG and LPG are considered cleaner fuels with lower greenhouse gas emissions, aiding in reducing air pollution and combating climate change. Additionally, these vehicles offer financial advantages such as lower fuel costs and tax incentives in some regions, making them attractive to consumers and fleet operators.</p><p>The global CNG and LPG vehicles market has witnessed steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.3% during the forecast period.
