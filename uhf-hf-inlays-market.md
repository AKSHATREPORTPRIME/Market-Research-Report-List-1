<p><p>HF (High Frequency) Inlays and UHF (Ultra High Frequency) Inlays are both types of radio frequency identification (RFID) technology used in various industries. HF inlays operate in the frequency range of 13.56 MHz and are commonly used for applications such as access control and contactless payment systems. On the other hand, UHF inlays operate at higher frequencies, ranging from 860 to 960 MHz, and are suitable for applications requiring longer read ranges, such as inventory management and supply chain tracking.
