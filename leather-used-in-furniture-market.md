<p><p>Leather used in furniture can be categorized into two main market types: real leather and faux leather. Real leather refers to furniture made from animal hide, such as cow or sheepskin. It is known for its durability, natural feel, and unique variations in texture and color. On the other hand, faux leather, also called synthetic or imitation leather, is a man-made material that replicates the appearance of real leather. It is typically more affordable, easy to clean, and offers a wide range of color options, making it an attractive alternative for budget-conscious consumers.</p></p>
