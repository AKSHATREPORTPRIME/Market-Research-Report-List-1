<p><p>Seed treatment biofertilizers refer to the application of beneficial microorganisms or substances on the seeds to enhance their growth and yield. Liquid biofertilizers are in liquid form and contain beneficial microorganisms such as nitrogen-fixing bacteria, phosphate solubilizing bacteria, etc. They are directly applied to the seeds. Carrier-based biofertilizers are formulated with solid carriers like peat, vermiculite, or clay. These carriers protect the microorganisms and ensure their sustained release. Both types of biofertilizers contribute to improving seed germination, nutrient uptake, and overall plant health.</p></p>
