<p><p>HVDC (High Voltage Direct Current) converter stations are crucial components of HVDC transmission systems used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) and vice versa for efficient long-distance power transmission. The market for HVDC converter stations can be categorized into different types, including monopolar, bi-polar, back-to-back, and multi-terminal. </p><p>A monopolar system involves a single DC transmission line with one converter station at the sending end and another at the receiving end. In a bi-polar system, two conductor systems are used with a converter station in between to connect them.</p><p>Back-to-back converter stations provide DC transmission in proximity where there is no need for transmission over long distances. They convert AC power from one system to DC and then back to AC of the desired frequency for interconnections.</p><p>Multi-terminal systems involve three or more converter stations interconnected by transmission lines, allowing the transmission of power among multiple points or systems. These converter stations come together to create a multi-terminal network, enhancing the reliability and flexibility of power transmission.</p></p>
