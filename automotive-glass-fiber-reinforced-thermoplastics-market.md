<p><p>The automotive glass fiber reinforced thermoplastics market offers two types of fibers - long fiber and short fiber. Long fiber thermoplastics (LFT) are made by impregnating continuous glass fibers with thermoplastic resins, resulting in enhanced strength and stiffness. LFT is mainly used for structural components in the automotive industry due to its high strength properties. On the other hand, short fiber thermoplastics (SFT) are created by incorporating short glass fibers into the thermoplastic matrix to enhance their mechanical properties. SFT is commonly used in interior automotive parts for their improved impact resistance and dimensional stability.</p></p>
