<p><p>The ion-exchange polymer market consists of two main types: anion ion-exchange polymers and cation ion-exchange polymers. Anion ion-exchange polymers possess negatively charged functional groups that can attract and exchange positively charged ions. They are commonly used in wastewater treatment, desalination processes, and purification of acidic solutions. On the other hand, cation ion-exchange polymers have positively charged functional groups that can attract and exchange negatively charged ions. They find applications in water softening, metal recovery, and purification of alkaline solutions. Both types of polymers play a vital role in various industrial processes requiring ion-exchange capabilities.</p></p>
