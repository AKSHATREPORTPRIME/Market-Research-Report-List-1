<p><p>Label Market Types:</p><p>1. Pressure Sensitive: Pressure-sensitive labels have an adhesive layer that sticks to surfaces upon application. They are versatile, suitable for various materials, and easy to apply.</p><p>2. Glue-Applied: Glue-applied labels require a specific adhesive or glue to be applied during the labeling process. They offer a reliable and permanent application, making them ideal for products that may be exposed to water, heat, or chemicals.</p><p>3. Heat-Shrink and Stretch Sleeve: These labels are made from plastic film that shrinks when heat is applied. They provide 360-degree coverage and are commonly used for labeling bottles, cans, and containers, ensuring a tight, secure fit.</p><p>4. In-Mold Labels: In-mold labels are applied during the manufacturing process, where the label is placed into the mold before the plastic is injected. This results in a seamless integration of label and packaging, providing durability and improved design options.</p><p>These different label market types offer distinct advantages for various applications, catering to specific labeling requirements across industries.</p></p>
