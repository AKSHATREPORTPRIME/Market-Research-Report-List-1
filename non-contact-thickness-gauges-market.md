X-Ray thickness gauges utilize high-energy X-rays to penetrate the material and measure its thickness. Infrared Ray gauges use non-ionizing radiation to detect the thickness of an object by analyzing the reflection and absorption of the rays. Others refer to alternative technologies like ultrasonic gauges that use sound waves to determine thickness. These gauges provide accurate and efficient measurements for various industries including manufacturing, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
