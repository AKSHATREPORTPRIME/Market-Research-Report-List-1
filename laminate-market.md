<p><ul><li>High Pressure Laminate</li><li>Low Pressure Laminate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Laminate market is divided into two main types: High Pressure Laminate (HPL) and Low Pressure Laminate (LPL). HPL, commonly used for countertops and furniture, is created by stacking multiple layers of resin-infused kraft paper and saturating them with phenolic resin. This type of laminate is highly durable and resistant to heat, scratches, and chemicals. On the other hand, LPL is created by applying several thin layers of decorative paper onto a substrate material, typically particleboard. LPL is more cost-effective and commonly used for flooring, wall panels, and cabinets.</p></p>
